Why Nashville

Music City was made for this

There's no better place to celebrate brotherhood than the live-music capital of the world. From the neon of Broadway to the smell of slow-smoked BBQ, every block of downtown Nashville is built for a great weekend with the brothers.

Music City
Live music spills out of every doorway. Honky tonks, blues bars, and rooftop stages — there's a soundtrack to every hour of the weekend.
Honky Tonk Highway
Lower Broadway is one long block party — Kid Rock's, Tootsie's, Robert's, AJ's. Our hotel sits right at the edge of it all.
Southern Eats
Hot chicken, smoked brisket, biscuits, and bourbon. Hattie B's, Martin's, Prince's — you won't go hungry, and you won't eat the same thing twice.
Everything's Walkable
From our hotel on Broadway, every bar, venue, and restaurant on this list is within a short walk. No long Ubers, no missing the action.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the dress code?
Cocktail attire with a Nashville twist throughout the weekend. Boots and pearl-snaps encouraged. Specifics will be posted closer to the trip.
How do I book my hotel room?
Reach out to the Formal Chair, Aidan Marchese, at (847) 507-8169 to book your room. Group rate info will be shared once finalized.
Is transportation provided?
No formal transportation is provided to Nashville — try to carpool with your brothers. Roster will be coordinated closer to the trip. Once at the hotel, everything on the itinerary is walking distance.
Who do I contact with questions?
Aidan Marchese, Formal Chair — (847) 507-8169. Contact info is also in the footer below.
What does my payment cover?
Hotel room (split among roommates) and group events organized by Fiji. Food, drinks, ubers, and personal spending are on you.
Can I request specific roommates?
Yes — send your preferred roommate list to Aidan when you submit your deposit. Final assignments will be confirmed before the trip.
What if I miss a payment deadline?
Reach out to Aidan as soon as possible. Late payments may forfeit your spot, and missed deadlines can mean your room is reassigned.
What should I pack?
Cocktail and Western attire for nights out, comfortable walking shoes for Broadway, layers for variable spring weather (highs in the 70s, lows in the 50s), and your ID.
Can I drive my own car instead of carpooling?
Yes, but valet at the hotel runs $40/night. Carpooling with brothers saves money and is more fun. Coordinate with Aidan if you plan to drive solo.
